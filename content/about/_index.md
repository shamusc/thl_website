  - block: markdown
    content:
      title: Why Turtle House Labs?
      text: |
        Turtle House Labs grew out of a practical and scientific question: how can sequencing data, ecological reasoning, and validation experiments be connected in a way that helps identify useful microbial candidates for crop disease suppression?

        The current focus is the phyllosphere microbiome and a translational framework for moving from observational data toward prioritized candidates and follow-up experiments. The motivating field-relevant system is apple foliar disease pressure, particularly cedar apple rust.

  - block: markdown
    content:
      title: Values
      text: |
        - **Scientific rigor.** Take biological complexity seriously and report uncertainty honestly.
        - **Practical translation.** Design analyses with downstream experiments and field use in mind.
        - **Ecological humility.** Microbial community data are hard to interpret. Association is not causation.
        - **Farmer-relevant outcomes.** The work should eventually be useful to people who manage crops.
        - **Open and reproducible methods** where possible.
        - **No overclaiming.** Frameworks and candidates are stated as such, not as validated solutions.
